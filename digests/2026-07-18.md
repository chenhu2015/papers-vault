Value-Gradient Hypothesis (0.87) is today's most significant paper. The RIPO paper (Jul 17) showed that PPO-Clip's Euclidean geometry is wrong for policy manifolds; this paper provides the complementary question: why do critic-free methods work at all without explicit value estimates? The answer — actor backward pass propagates costates whose conditional expectation equals the value gradient — is non-obvious and provides the theoretical foundation for the entire GRPO family. The sampling gap and policy entropy error bound also suggests why diversity-preserving RL methods (ARMOR, DISA, EGRSD) improve performance: they reduce the approximation error in the empirical costate.

IGRPO (0.85) extends the agentic TTS thread (TTI, Scaling TTS for Agents) to a new level: TTI showed curriculum RL on rollout *horizon* is the right TTS axis for agents; IGRPO shows that within that horizon, *which branches* to expand via information gain is equally critical. The induced teacher distribution unification is the cleanest theoretical integration between tree-search-based exploration and RL policy optimization in the vault. IGRPO and TTI together represent a complete two-level decomposition of agentic compute allocation: how many interactions (TTI) and which branches (IGRPO).

RL-ZVP (0.84) closes a structural gap in GRPO that the vault has not previously identified: roughly half of all training prompts are discarded because they produce zero-variance advantage estimates. The entropy-guided token modulation connects to EGRSD's entropy gate from Jul 17, but from the opposite direction — EGRSD weights uncertain tokens *down* (in OPD), while RL-ZVP weights uncertain tokens to preserve nuanced signals (in RLVR). Together they define a consistent principle: entropy of the policy at a token position is an informative signal about training value, and both up-weighting and down-weighting strategies can be principled depending on the regime.

Paradox of Outcome Optimization (0.82) provides the missing theoretical complement to the vault's empirical PRM thread (GroundedPRM, KV-PRM, SCI-PRM, LLMs-as-a-Jury). The Semantic Coverage Measure (η) bound is the most practically actionable finding: it shifts the question from "how much data?" to "how diverse is the semantic coverage?" — explaining why scaling homogeneous benchmarks fails to fix OOD brittleness. The PRMs-as-Topological-Filters formalization also elevates the theoretical status of process supervision from "useful heuristic" to "mathematically necessary for causal generalization."

Where Should RL Compute Go (0.80) provides the highest-level perspective of any paper in the vault: not how to improve a specific component (RIPO: geometry, RL-ZVP: zero-variance prompts, IGRPO: branch selection) but how to allocate the entire FLOP budget across components. The coupling insight — model size and training allocation cannot be decided independently — is the most practical finding: it means that comparing papers that use different model sizes and report only accuracy is insufficient; FLOP breakdown is necessary for fair comparison.

**Emerging thread — theoretical foundations of GRPO (new):** Three papers now provide theoretical grounding for the GRPO optimization internals thread opened Jul 17: RIPO (geometric fix for PPO-Clip manifold), Value-Gradient Hypothesis (why critic-free gradient carries value signal), and Paradox of Outcome Optimization (why outcome optimization produces shortcut solutions and why PRMs fix it). Together they form a coherent theoretical framework: (1) the policy lives on a Riemannian manifold not Euclidean space (RIPO), (2) the RL gradient still carries value signal despite no critic because backward propagation through attention approximates value gradient (Value-Gradient Hypothesis), (3) outcome-only rewards induce Markovian Screening of causal reasoning chains — PRMs are topologically necessary for OOD generalization (Paradox of Outcome Optimization).

**Updated thread — data efficiency in RL training:** RL-ZVP (zero-variance prompts recovered), RSPO (reward-swap for sampling efficiency), Prune-OPD (token-level truncation), TurnOPD (turn-level truncation), EGRSD (entropy-gated token weighting), IGRPO (information gain branch allocation). The unified principle across all six: the default uniform treatment of all training data, tokens, and branches in GRPO wastes a large fraction of compute, and principled non-uniform allocation consistently improves efficiency and performance.
