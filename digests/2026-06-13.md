**All 5 digest papers from queries 3 and 4.** CUA-Gym (0.85) is the standout: the first large-scale RLVR dataset explicitly for computer-use agents — the Generator-Discriminator-Orchestrator co-generation architecture is a direct adaptation of SENTINEL's controller-proposer-solver pattern to environment *synthesis* rather than failure-driven task generation. PRISM (0.83) reveals a structural flaw that has been silently corrupting PRM training across the field: step-level class imbalance causes CE to overcredit wrong steps, and the FP asymmetry result (false positives hurt search quality more than false negatives) provides the principled reason why this matters more than it looks. Reasoning Arena (0.82) is the reward-side complement to Query Recycling (Jun 11): where Query Recycling recycles zero-variance prompts from the sampling side, Reasoning Arena replaces collapsed scalar rewards with a preference signal on those same groups — the two approaches could be stacked. LiteGUI (0.82) finds that small GUI models fail from SFT policy rigidity, not capacity limits — a finding that mirrors FORT-Searcher's (Jun 11) result that data quality matters more than algorithm for search agents. Representation-Aware Advantage (0.82) rounds out the reward signal cluster: PRISM fixes training bias, HARVE edits hacking-susceptible reward heads, and RAAE extracts more information from the existing RM at inference time — three orthogonal layers in the reward quality stack.
