OpenForgeRL (0.88) provides the missing training infrastructure for harness-based agentic RL. The proxy-intercepts-harness-calls design means any agent built on Claude Code, Codex, or similar harnesses can now be trained with standard RL (veRL, etc.) without modifying the harness. Combined with ToolVerse (also today), these papers answer the "how do you scale agentic RL?" question from two orthogonal directions: infrastructure (OpenForgeRL: how do rollouts get recorded?) and environment (ToolVerse: what do agents train on at scale?). The harness-choice-affects-trainability finding from OpenForgeRL is a new variable not yet in the vault: the inference scaffold is not neutral with respect to RL learning dynamics, and some harnesses may be systematically harder to improve via RL — this connects to the GRPO web agent failure paper's headroom principle (Jul 22) but adds a structural dimension (harness design, not just task difficulty).

Clip-Low/Clip-High (0.85) fully closes the asymmetric clipping thread queued from Jul 23. The theoretical proof establishes that PPO/GRPO's clip-low and clip-high are not symmetric regularizers — they have opposite effects on policy entropy, and under standard parameters the entropy-decreasing clip-high effect dominates. This means entropy collapse is partly a clipping artifact, not purely a reward-signal phenomenon. The implication is that the GRPO web agent failure (Jul 22) and the RL-ZVP zero-variance starvation (earlier) may have been compounded by clipping-induced entropy collapse, independently of reward headroom. GFlowRL's asymmetric flow-gap clipping (Jul 23) and DISPO's asymmetric IS clipping now have a direct mechanistic parallel: all three papers suppress one direction of gradient updates asymmetrically, and this paper reveals the entropy consequences of that choice in the PPO/GRPO case.

OPPO (0.84) is the most principled solution to the credit assignment problem surfaced so far. GRPO assigns trajectory-level advantage uniformly to all tokens; TACO/EGRSD identify high-credit tokens via heuristic or counterfactual signals; OPPO provides the first information-theoretic derivation: the Bayesian posterior over eventual success is a closed-form recursion of the oracle signal, and its derivative at each token is the token-level advantage. The self-oracle variant recovers on-policy distillation reward as a special case, unifying the distillation-style and GRPO-style approaches. The state-weight term (which concentrates advantage on pivotal tokens) is analogous to BPO's entropy-weighted branching (which concentrates rollout budget on high-uncertainty decision points): both papers identify that not all positions in a trajectory contribute equally and allocate learning resources accordingly.

APO (0.82) closes the persistent asymmetric correct/incorrect treatment gap (open since Jul 15, gap item #7). The DADS mechanism (difficulty-scaled KL for positives) and STCR mechanism (length penalty for negatives) are structurally independent interventions, not a single asymmetric advantage function — this is different from DISPO (asymmetric IS clipping on negative advantages), AsyPPO (asymmetric mini-critic coverage), and Clip-Low/Clip-High (asymmetric entropy effects). APO's multimodal setting is the first asymmetric treatment specifically studied in the VLM reasoning domain. The finding that overthinking (long negatives) is a separate failure mode from entropy collapse (short positives) is a key diagnostic for multimodal RL: the two failure modes pull in opposite directions (length), requiring asymmetric correction rather than a single regularizer.

ToolVerse (0.82) addresses the environment side of agentic RL scale. The tool-dependency-graph approach to long-horizon task generation is structurally novel: prior agentic RL benchmarks (WebShop, ALFWorld, SWE-bench) have fixed task horizons and fixed action spaces; ToolVerse's graph encodes prerequisite tool calls, making horizon length and task complexity emergent from tool dependencies. The Turn-Aware Relative Advantage algorithm is ToolVerse's answer to the same multi-step credit problem that BPO (prefix branching), HPO (Wasserstein intent-space), and OPPO (Bayesian recursion) address via different mechanisms — this is the 4th distinct approach to agentic credit assignment in the vault.
