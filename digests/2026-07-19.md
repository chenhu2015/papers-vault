SD-MAR (0.83) is today's most immediately practical paper. GRPO-lite with Backward Discounted Allocation (BDA) addresses a structural problem in multi-step visual reasoning: standard GRPO weights all reasoning steps equally, but in multi-image comparison tasks, the analytical conclusion forms at the end of the chain and should receive higher credit. BDA formalizes this by applying a backward discount — later steps receive higher weight. This is the step-level credit assignment analogue to the token-level (EGRSD) and turn-level (TurnOPD) weighting papers already in the vault. SD-MAR completes a four-level credit assignment hierarchy: token (EGRSD, Prune-OPD), turn (TurnOPD), reasoning-step (SD-MAR/BDA), and branch (IGRPO). Each level has now been addressed independently.

GEOALIGN (0.82) adds a new signal for rollout quality: not reward magnitude, not difficulty, not entropy — but geometric consensus in representation space. The directional inconsistency failure mode is distinct from the noisy-reward problem (RSPO), the zero-variance problem (RL-ZVP), and the curriculum difficulty problem (DeReason). Rollouts that are high-reward but geometrically inconsistent with the batch cause destabilizing updates; replacing them with stable within-prompt alternatives preserves the reward signal without the variance spike. This is the cleanest latent-space-based data curation method in the vault.

GeoMin (0.81) closes the queued gap from Jul 18 on data quality in RLVR: not by filtering labeled data, but by assessing the reliability of self-reward signals on unlabeled data. The geometric prior derived from labeled correct/incorrect rollout distributions is used to score whether a given unlabeled rollout's self-generated reward is reliable. 10% annotation matching full supervision is the headline result, but the deeper finding is that structural discrepancy between correct and incorrect rollouts is a learnable and transferable signal.

CORA (0.81) identifies a failure mode in multimodal RLVR not previously characterized in the vault: the reasoning trace and the final answer can be semantically inconsistent even when the answer is correct. This matters because training on inconsistent trace-answer pairs teaches the model that incoherent reasoning is acceptable. The plug-and-play consistency reward model is the practical contribution; HRAS is the engineering fix that prevents the consistency reward from crowding out the task reward.

DISPO (0.80) completes a structural analysis of REINFORCE-style IS weight clipping that the vault had only partially covered. RIPO addressed policy-space geometry (Euclidean vs. Riemannian for PPO-Clip); DISPO addresses the within-policy-update asymmetry between correct and incorrect response treatment. The four-regime decomposition (correct/incorrect × up/down clip) is the key insight: PPO/GRPO's current approaches conflate regimes with conflicting optimal clip values. DISPO's +5.62% over CISPO on AIME'24 is the strongest single-paper gain in the vault's optimization internals thread.
