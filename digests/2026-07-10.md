The OOD transfer angle (search 4) was the most productive new direction.

"When RL Suppresses Its Own Vocabulary" (0.88) is the most significant paper today and the most novel contribution to the vault this week. It identifies the seventh RLVR failure mode — *exploratory primitive suppression* — operating at a different level from the six prior modes in the vault: this one is about the model's *behavioral vocabulary* being narrowed by RL to a single compute-verify mode, even as performance improves. The puzzle-to-math transfer recipe (no math in training, 16%→36% hard-math pass@32) is the most dramatic cross-domain transfer result in the vault. The novelty bonus (perplexity under reference model) provides a practical, reward-model-free mechanism for restoring exploratory diversity.

ROSE (0.82) closes the loop between two threads: tree-based exploration (MCTS from general RL) and semantic diversity in RLVR rollouts (TA-GRPO, Reaching Beyond the Mode). The semantic-entropy branching strategy is the structural complement to the novelty bonus: both target rollout diversity, but ROSE acts at search-time branching points while the novelty bonus acts at the training objective level. The length-aware segment-level advantage is also the third independent implementation of sub-response-level advantage estimation after MRPO (step-position) and GRPO-λ (eligibility traces), adding a length-aware efficiency dimension neither prior paper addressed.

EPPO (0.82) adds the multi-task dimension to the entropy-adaptive control thread. The inter-task entropy crossover failure mode is distinct from all six prior modes: it operates at the *task distribution* level (easy tasks crowding out harder tasks' exploration) rather than within a single trajectory or campaign. Task-wise dynamic clipping is also the fourth variant of entropy-adaptive GRPO updates after EGSPO (token-level gating), AdaPrefix-GRPO (success-rate feedback controller), and ADS (semantic-cluster distribution selection) — together these four papers cover entropy/difficulty control at four granularities: token, problem, semantic cluster, and task.

SR-GRPO (0.78) is the most surprising paper today: internal model geometry (stable rank) as a reward signal achieves 84% on RewardBench without any human labels or reward model, matching many supervised baselines. This closes the reward-source taxonomy: the vault now has verifiable outcome rewards (RLVR), human preference labels (RLHF), LLM examiner (LLM-as-a-Tutor), and internal geometry (SR-GRPO). The connection to EGSPO is important: both use information-theoretic signals from model internals, but at different points in the update pipeline — SR-GRPO uses stable rank at the sequence level to score outputs, EGSPO uses per-token entropy to gate gradient updates.

UnityMAS-O (0.75) completes the agentic RL infrastructure stack. Together with DynaWeb (environment) and ARTIST (single-agent multi-turn tool), the vault now has a full three-layer view: world model, single-agent policy, multi-agent workflow optimizer. The role-specific credit at role/turn/trajectory levels is the workflow-level extension of the credit assignment thread (MRPO, GRPO-λ, TAPO, TRIAGE, CRAFT).

**Emerging framework — RLVR failure mode taxonomy (updated):** Seven candidate failure modes now catalogued: (1) information self-locking / SeL, (2) diversity/gradient collapse (TA-GRPO), (3) capability boundary collapse (RL-PLUS), (4) view-dependent advantage collapse (ConsistRoll), (5) rise-and-collapse / within-task over-optimization (Self-Regress), (6) answer-distribution collapse (Reaching Beyond the Mode), (7) exploratory primitive suppression (When RL Suppresses Its Own Vocabulary). Mode 7 is the first mode operating at the behavioral vocabulary level — it can co-exist with performance gains, making it uniquely hard to detect without the reasoning primitive framework.

**Emerging framework — rollout diversity mechanisms:** Four mechanisms now in the vault: TA-GRPO (question rephrasing, input level), Reaching Beyond the Mode (multi-answer RL, output distribution level), ROSE (semantic-entropy branching, MCTS search level), novelty bonus in "When RL Suppresses..." (perplexity-based training objective level). A unified framework would combine semantic-entropy branching during rollout (ROSE) with a novelty bonus during training (today's paper).

**Emerging framework — entropy-adaptive GRPO variants:** Four papers, four granularities: EGSPO (token level), AdaPrefix-GRPO (problem level), ADS (semantic cluster level), EPPO (task level). A synthesis would layer all four — token gating for credit precision, problem-level prefix for curriculum, cluster distribution for data scheduling, task-level clipping for multi-task stability.
