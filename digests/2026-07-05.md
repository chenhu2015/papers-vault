The on-policy distillation sub-thread (MOPD, GNDPO, Visual-OPSD) emerged unexpectedly as the most productive angle — three papers that collectively address multi-teacher capability integration, training stability, and VLM-specific distillation cost.

CF-GRPO (0.82) is the most direct extension of the VLM process reward thread. Where VisCritic (Jul 4) derived process reward from GUI screenshot comparison and ReasonRAG from RAG sub-task roles, CF-GRPO derives process reward from intrinsic video structure — temporal coverage, scene transitions, and query relevance — and expresses it as a frame-use agreement objective in GRPO. The temporal-annotation-free property is the key practical contribution: it removes the labeling bottleneck that has prevented video RL training from reaching the density of process supervision available in text or GUI domains.

AREW/SeL (0.78) opens a new framing distinct from all prior credit assignment papers in the vault. Every prior paper (TRIAGE, CRAFT, UCOB, OPID, etc.) assumed that informative trajectories exist and asked how to assign credit within them. SeL asks what happens when outcome-based RL prevents informative trajectories from being generated at all — the credit assignment problem and the exploration problem are coupled, not independent. AREW's directional critique reweighting is the first credit-adjacent remedy that targets this pre-trajectory failure mode, making it a structural complement to the seven credit assignment axes rather than an eighth axis.

MOPD (0.75) extends the on-policy distillation thread from single-teacher (GTR-Turbo, CRAFT) to multi-teacher. The MiMo-V2-Flash deployment confirms industrial-scale validity. The parallel, independent teacher development property is the practical advance: it decouples capability development timelines, which has been the main bottleneck in multi-domain post-training.

GNDPO (0.75) closes a stability gap in the on-policy distillation literature. The normalization-as-stabilization insight is structurally the same principle GRPO applies to verifiable rewards, now applied within the distillation signal itself — gradient explosions from outlier KL states are the distillation analog of the reward scale sensitivity that GRPO normalization addresses.

Visual-OPSD (0.72) removes the generation-cost bottleneck for VLM on-policy distillation. The finding that the generation pathway encodes useful reasoning beyond the rendered pixels (confirmed by KL diagnostic, 58.4% KL gap closure) justifies reusing SFT-time VTs as teacher context during RL — a one-time cost instead of per-round regeneration.

**Emerging thread:** on-policy distillation is converging toward a unified post-training paradigm for both LLMs and VLMs: (1) generate student rollouts, (2) compute a dense signal from a privileged teacher view (checkpoint, VTs, or domain expert), (3) normalize that signal at the batch level (GNDPO), (4) apply to student parameters without allowing the teacher to drive gradients directly (CRAFT's design rule, MOPD's separation). GNDPO's normalization and MOPD's multi-teacher generalization are the two most missing pieces in the existing literature, and both appear today.
