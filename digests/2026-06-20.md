**All 5 papers from queries 2 and 4.** Query 1 (GRPO zero-gradient) is confirmed structurally broken at title level — the "group" + "advantage" combination attracts physics/math literature. Query 3 (verifier agent) too sparse and off-target.

ReasonMaxxer (0.78) is the standout: the "1-3% of tokens at high-entropy branching points" finding provides the unifying empirical theory for the entire credit assignment thread. AT-RL (Jun 14) found ~15% anchor tokens in multimodal settings; ProFIL (today) zeros advantage for post-commitment theater starting from a commitment point; STRIDE (today) contrasts trajectories at strategic n-gram positions. All three are consequences of the same structure: effective RL intervention is sparse, concentrated at high-entropy positions where the model commits to a reasoning branch. The "sparse policy selection, not capability learning" framing also gives the mechanistic reason why DRE's trajectory editing works (the post-commitment segment has already passed all high-entropy positions) and why ARBOR's zero-gradient problem arises (if the group is outcome-homogeneous, all members chose the same branch at the critical entropy positions).

STRIDE (0.82) is the strongest new method: it is the first to use within-GRPO-group trajectory contrast as the credit signal itself, deriving discriminative n-gram patterns rather than importing an external rubric or probe. The connection to Reasoning Arena (Jun 13, trace tournaments) is structural — both exploit within-group trajectory comparisons — but STRIDE operates at sub-trajectory n-gram granularity and assigns differentiated advantages rather than replacing the scalar reward. Together they define a natural stack: Reasoning Arena provides a group-level preference when scalar rewards collapse to zero, STRIDE provides n-gram-level differentiation within groups that *have* non-uniform outcomes.

ProFIL (0.80) completes the trajectory credit stack: DRE (Jun 19) edits successful-trajectory post-answer suffixes before the RL update, STRIDE (today) assigns discriminative credit during the update, ProFIL (today) zeroes advantage for detected theater rollouts during the update. The frozen-base probe design is a clean engineering insight: grounding the theater detector in the pre-RL base avoids the Goodhart dynamic where the RL policy learns to fool an in-training probe.

SPS (0.77) + GOLF (0.77) together define the exploration frontier: SPS addresses the distribution-level failure (probability squeezing), GOLF addresses the signal-level failure (scalar reward inadequacy in sparse regions). The SPS "squeezing effect" also provides the missing mechanistic link between ARBOR's zero-gradient problem (Jun 19) and the broader exploration thread: squeezing causes outcome-homogeneous groups by concentrating the policy on a narrow trajectory manifold.
