**All 5 papers from searches 3, 4, and 5.** Searches 1 and 2 confirmed off-target. The exploration failure mode sub-thread (TA-GRPO, RL-PLUS, Attention as Compass) emerged as the most productive angle across three papers — together they complete a triad of distinct RLVR pathologies: diversity collapse (TA-GRPO), capability boundary collapse (RL-PLUS), and PSRL branching inefficiency (Attention as Compass). Nemotron-Cascade 2 is the industrial validation of MOPD's per-domain teacher architecture.

Nemotron-Cascade 2 (0.82) answers MOPD's open question about *when* to stop domain teacher training: the answer is to distill continuously from intermediate checkpoints throughout cascade RL rather than training domain teachers to convergence first. The intermediate-as-teacher strategy turns potential regression events (when adding new RL domains destabilizes prior capabilities) into distillation triggers. This is a cleaner operational policy than MOPD's implicit "train until convergence" framing and is validated at IMO/IOI/ICPC gold medal level.

TA-GRPO (0.75) and RL-PLUS (0.72) are structurally complementary to AREW/SeL (Jul 5) and form a three-paper triad of RLVR failure modes: SeL is about reward signal preventing informative trajectory generation (pre-trajectory failure), diversity collapse is about group-level gradient degeneracy within those trajectories (mid-training failure), and capability boundary collapse is about on-policy constraint narrowing the problem scope (training-scope failure). All three failure modes can co-occur, and none of the three remedies (AREW directional reweighting, TA-GRPO rephrasing, RL-PLUS hybrid-policy) addresses the other two — suggesting a composite remedy combining all three is the natural next step.

Sentence-level Reward Model (0.75) fills the granularity gap in the reward model hierarchy. The vault's credit assignment thread has covered response-level (RLHF baseline), role-typed segment (TRIAGE), token-level (AT-RL, TAPO), and context-view credit (UCOB/OPID), but sentence-level preference reward — the RLHF-side analog of step-level process supervision — was absent. The differential boundary approach is the preference alignment counterpart of process reward model design, making it the natural bridge between the credit assignment thread and the RLHF thread from the interest profile.

Attention as a Compass (0.71) bridges the process supervision and exploration threads: attention patterns as branching-position signal is the PSRL-specific analog of BiPACE's (Jul 3) insight that the policy's own hidden-state geometry should define state partitions for advantage estimation. Both papers use intrinsic model geometry to replace an externally specified structure (BiPACE replaces a manually designed state taxonomy; Attention as Compass replaces a manually designed branching curriculum).

**Emerging framework — RLVR failure modes:** Three independent failure modes now have named remedies in the vault: (1) information self-locking → AREW directional critique reweighting, (2) diversity collapse / gradient vanishing → TA-GRPO rephrasing, (3) capability boundary collapse → RL-PLUS hybrid-policy. A fourth mode (branching inefficiency in PSRL) has Attention as Compass as a partial remedy. The natural research question is whether these failure modes are independent or whether fixing one amplifies or suppresses another — no paper in the vault addresses their interaction.
