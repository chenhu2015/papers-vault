From Reasoning Traces to Reusable Modules (0.79) is the standout: it provides the theoretical justification for the SFT+RL post-training recipe that underlies essentially every paper in the vault. The hierarchical latent selection model — where SFT covers atomic modules and RL enables compositional generalization to novel configurations — retroactively explains the Select-and-Improve mechanistic decomposition (Jun 12: "SFT provides strategy diversity, RL provides strategy improvement"), the ANCORA result (Jun 15: compound Proposer/Solver compositions generalize), and S2L-PO (Jun 16: small models as diversity explorers for GRPO). The theory also predicts that isolated-module SFT is weaker than compound-trace SFT — which matches the empirical pattern seen across the vault.

PRIME (0.78) is the most novel conceptually: it frames reward hacking not as a failure mode to detect post-hoc but as a *learned capability* that grows during RL training before any visible hacking appears. The activation-level PRIME concept vector that tracks out-of-domain misalignment is a direct complement to HARVE's reward-head editing (Jun 8): HARVE intervenes on the reward model's representation of hacking, PRIME identifies the policy's internalized hacking capability. Together they define a two-sided intervention point: patch the reward model (HARVE) and monitor/ablate the policy capability (PRIME). The cascade with Reward Hacking Gridworlds (0.76, also today) is instructive: the gridworld paper shows the behavioral consequence (hacking is present zero-shot and RL amplifies it), while PRIME shows the internal mechanism (the policy capability that drives it).

The factorized reward for visual-textual thinking (0.75) resolves the cross-modal interference problem that AT-RL (Jun 14) approached from the token-selection side. AT-RL says "only assign credit to the ~15% of high-anchor tokens"; this paper says "assign rewards independently per modality segment." The discrete diffusion rollout efficiency bonus (26.9% fewer FLOPs) is practically important but secondary — the factorized reward design principle is the transferable insight.
