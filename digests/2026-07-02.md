**All 5 papers from queries 3 and 4.** Queries 1 and 2 confirmed off-target at title level. Query 3 (agentic credit assignment) was the strongest, yielding 4 of 5 papers. The credit assignment thread has now extended from text-RL math benchmarks (FlowTracer, SCPO, GraphGPO) to agentic tool-use environments (TRIAGE, CRAFT, RewardFlow, DuCA).

TRIAGE (0.92) is the structural standout: it names regression-in-success and exploration-in-failure as the two precise blind spots of outcome-only GRPO credit, then corrects both with a role-conditioned rule (no learned reward model required). The theoretical result — role-conditioned credit is the optimal projection of per-segment advantage residual onto the role variable — provides the same kind of principled foundation that ACPO (Jul 1) gave for adaptive clipping. TRIAGE adds the sixth axis to the credit assignment hierarchy: rollout state graph (GraphGPO), attention DAG (FlowTracer), sibling-step (SCPO), trajectory-segment (DRE), token-level (AT-RL), and now role-typed action segment (TRIAGE).

CRAFT (0.88) is the closest complement to SCPO from the signal side: where SCPO borrows credit from semantically similar successful siblings to recover failed-step value, CRAFT estimates the counterfactual change in group advantage from upweighting teacher-preferred tokens — using the same G-1 sibling rollouts GRPO already computes. Pillar 2 (asymmetric KL/distillation controller) and Pillar 3 (polarized per-token KL) address the same channel contamination and resolution mismatch failure modes that PASS (Jul 1) diagnosed at the process signal input layer, but at the optimization layer. The bit-exact reproducibility guarantee (each pillar independently switchable) makes this the most rigorously isolated credit study in the digest so far.

GTR-Turbo (0.83) closes the loop opened by "Why Does RL Generalize Better Than SFT?" (Jul 1): that paper showed RL's VLM generalization advantage is data-selective, implying RL-distilled data pipelines may be replaceable with offline curation. GTR-Turbo confirms this direction for VLM agents — the merged checkpoint is essentially an offline curation of diverse RL policy states. The 50% wall-clock / 60% compute reduction also makes GTR-Turbo the most practical scaling result in the VLM agentic RL thread.

RewardFlow (0.82) is GraphGPO's (Jun 30) closest structural cousin but with a key directional distinction: GraphGPO's cross-trajectory goal-distance estimation is prospective (how far from success?), RewardFlow's topology-aware propagation is retrospective (how much did this state contribute?). The +29.7% gain on visual reasoning adds a concrete VLM agentic benchmark result to complement GTR-Turbo's training efficiency gains, making today's digest a paired VLM agentic contribution.

DuCA (0.75) operationalizes the multi-timescale credit problem at the episode horizon: where PASS diagnosed channel contamination between process and outcome signals at the token level, DuCA separates normalization across turn-level and session-level horizons before gradient fusion. The industrial sales evaluation (non-verifiable session-level outcomes) provides the most realistic non-math GRPO evaluation in the vault to date.

Tomorrow: (1) TRIAGE's four-category role taxonomy was designed and validated for navigation/search/shopping agentic tasks — there may be work on extending role-typed credit to code generation or tool-augmented reasoning agents where the role categories differ (e.g., hypothesis, verification, synthesis): `ti:(role OR segment OR step type OR action type) AND ti:(credit OR reward OR advantage) AND ti:(code OR programming OR tool) AND ti:(reinforcement OR RL OR GRPO) AND ti:(language model OR LLM OR agent)`. (2) CRAFT's counterfactual credit reuses G-1 GRPO rollouts — there may be analogous work on PPO-based agents that need to construct counterfactuals without group rollouts, or work comparing GRPO-style group counterfactuals vs. value-function-based counterfactuals in agentic settings: `ti:(counterfactual OR causal credit OR advantage decomposition) AND ti:(policy gradient OR PPO OR GRPO OR actor critic) AND ti:(agentic OR multi-step OR long-horizon) AND ti:(language model OR LLM OR agent)`. (3) RewardFlow + GraphGPO together establish graph-based state-level credit as a productive thread for annotation-free dense rewards — there may be work on combining both (prospective goal-distance + retrospective contribution propagation) in a unified graph framework: `ti:(state graph OR trajectory graph OR rollout graph OR reward propagation) AND ti:(reinforcement OR RL OR GRPO) AND ti:(agentic OR multi-step OR tool) AND ti:(language model OR LLM OR agent)`.
