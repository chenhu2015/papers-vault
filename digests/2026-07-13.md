RL Tango (0.90) is the most significant paper today. It answers the RL^V question at two levels: (1) empirically — co-evolved generative verifier achieves SOTA on ProcessBench; (2) mechanistically — the verifier generalizes OOD because it co-evolves with the generator via RL (not fixed SFT), so no frozen decision boundary for the policy to exploit. From Accuracy to Robustness (0.85) provides the complementary negative result: both rule-based verifiers (false negatives compound as policy strengthens) and SFT model verifiers (reward hacking) degrade RL training in distinct ways. Together these two papers close the RL^V verifier generalization question: the answer is generative co-training via RL.

PAG (0.82) adds the selective revision mechanism: rather than always generating a second attempt (as in RL^V's parallel sampling), PAG only revises when the model's own generative verification flags an error. This is the most compute-efficient point on the generator-verifier design space: TTS only when needed. When to Solve/Verify (0.80) provides the budget analysis that frames when verification is worth the cost at all: below 8x compute overhead, SC dominates; above 8x, GenRM can outperform.

Turn-Level Reward (0.80) closes the HRM → agentic multi-turn extension question. HRM provides hierarchical reward at the step level for math PRMs; this paper provides turn-level reward for multi-turn search agents, directly extending the credit assignment thread to the agentic tool-use setting. The two reward types (verifiable per-turn and LLM-as-judge) also map onto the open gap in DART/ARTIST: tool-use agentic RL needs per-turn credit, which this paper shows is achievable with either rule-based or model-based turn signals.

**Emerging framework — verifier co-training in RL (completed):** Four papers now characterize the full verifier design space: RL^V (co-train generative verifier for TTS, asymmetric training), Tango (symmetric co-evolution of generator + generative process verifier via RL), PAG (same model as policy + selective generative verifier in multi-turn loop), From Accuracy to Robustness (empirical failure modes of rule-based and SFT verifiers). The design insight across all four: verifier training must co-evolve with the policy via RL to avoid the rule-based false-negative and SFT reward-hacking failure modes documented here.
