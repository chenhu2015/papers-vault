AdaPrefix-GRPO (0.85) is the most significant paper today. It identifies and directly addresses GRPO's dead-zone failure mode — when all rollouts in a group fail, the group-relative advantage vanishes and the hardest problems contribute zero gradient. The feedback-controller framing is the key contribution: prior prefix-based methods (RePO, reference-guided approaches) set prefix length once; AdaPrefix continuously adjusts it to hold per-problem success near 50%, the point of maximum GRPO signal. This is a principled solution to a fundamental GRPO limitation that is separate from all six failure modes currently catalogued in the vault — it is a training-efficiency failure mode (curriculum collapse), not a generalization or optimization failure mode.

ADS (0.80) establishes the dual-level curriculum as a general RL post-training strategy: semantic clustering at the macro level to prevent training fixation, policy-boundary selection at the micro level to maximize advantage informativeness. The 5.2% gain across 7 benchmarks and 3 model sizes is unusually robust for a data scheduling method, suggesting the uniform sampling baseline is significantly suboptimal. Together with AdaPrefix-GRPO, the vault now has two complementary curriculum approaches: AdaPrefix targets per-problem difficulty calibration during generation, ADS targets data selection from the replay buffer.

DynaWeb (0.78) introduces the model-based RL approach to web agents — the key insight is that live web interaction is the bottleneck, and a learned web world model can substitute for most of the interaction cost. The random interleaving of expert trajectories with on-policy rollouts is the same hybrid strategy as BORA (VLA) and ReLook (agentic coding), suggesting this offline-anchoring pattern is converging as the standard stabilization technique in agentic RL training.

LLM-as-a-Tutor (0.75) is the non-verifiable RL counterpart to AdaPrefix's adaptive curriculum: in verifiable RL, task success rate directly signals when a prompt is too easy; in non-verifiable instruction-following, the LLM examiner's pairwise rollout comparison substitutes. The append-only atomic constraint design is the key engineering insight — it guarantees monotonic difficulty increase without risk of free-form rewriting introducing semantic drift or reversing previous constraints.

ARTIST (0.75) establishes outcome-based RL as sufficient for multi-turn tool integration — step-level supervision is not required. This is the agentic analog of RLVR's key insight (verifiable outcome rewards suffice for complex reasoning), applied to the tool selection and invocation problem. Combined with AutoTool (invocation decision, mode-selective) and DynaWeb (model-based web environment), the vault now has three papers addressing complementary facets of agentic tool-use RL: when to use tools (AutoTool), how to learn tool use without step supervision (ARTIST), and how to learn tool environments without live interaction (DynaWeb).

**Emerging framework — adaptive curriculum for GRPO:** Three papers now form a complementary set: AdaPrefix-GRPO (per-problem difficulty via prefix length, feedback controller), ADS (data distribution via semantic clustering + policy boundary), DUMP (distribution-level UCB scheduling from earlier vault). The common insight is that uniform sampling is a significant bottleneck in GRPO post-training, but each paper operates at a different granularity — individual problem, semantic cluster, and training distribution respectively.

**Emerging framework — agentic tool-use RL:** The vault now has three papers on complementary facets of tool use in agentic LLMs: AutoTool (invocation decision: tool vs. no-tool with dual-mode reward), ARTIST (full multi-turn pipeline: when/how/which tool via outcome-based RL), DynaWeb (environment modeling: learn a web world model to replace live interaction). A natural synthesis would combine DynaWeb's world model with AutoTool's invocation-mode reward signal and ARTIST's pipeline-level outcome reward — none of the three papers addresses all three facets simultaneously.
