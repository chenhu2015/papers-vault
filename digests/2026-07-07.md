AutoTool (0.80) is the most operationally significant paper today. It reframes tool use in agentic MLLMs from a binary on/off decision to a dual-mode RL problem with mode-specific rewards, directly addressing the over-invocation failure mode that prior work on tool-augmented reasoning implicitly ignores. The 44.9% efficiency gain on POPE confirms that unnecessary tool calls are a real efficiency bottleneck in agentic systems, not just a correctness issue. The joint multi-mode exploration strategy is structurally parallel to RL-PLUS's hybrid-policy (Jul 6), which mixed on- and off-policy data to prevent capability boundary collapse — both papers treat mode switching as a meta-level RL problem rather than a fixed design choice.

ConsistRoll (0.76) extends the RLVR failure mode framework by identifying a fourth distinct failure mode: view-dependent advantage collapse, where GRPO converges to solutions that exploit specific visual rendering artifacts rather than semantic content. The cross-view joint reward mechanism is structurally equivalent to TA-GRPO's question rephrasing (both create a consistency constraint between semantically equivalent inputs at the group level) but applied to visual input space rather than question-text space. This orthogonality suggests a composite training objective combining TA-GRPO rephrasing + ConsistRoll cross-view consistency could address both textual and visual diversity collapse simultaneously.

Segmenting Text (0.75) closes a second slot in the reward granularity hierarchy. Together with the sentence-level reward model from Jul 6, the vault now has two independent implementations of sub-response RLHF: one defined by sentence structural boundaries (differential boundary + attention aggregation), one by semantic segment completeness (dynamic segmentation + location-aware normalizers). The location-aware normalizer is the key practical contribution: it generalizes the scalar bandit normalizer to handle the fact that segment positions in a response have different statistical properties (early segments are shorter and more structurally constrained, late segments are more semantically resolved).

MRPO (0.72) adds step-position-weighted exponential credit to the credit assignment hierarchy, making it the temporal-position analog of the geometric discount in RL value functions applied to reasoning steps. The cascade failure diagnosis is independently valuable: MRPO reports that 64% of incorrect predictions in medical VQA involve early-step failures, which means that the majority of learning signal in outcome-centric training (standard GRPO) is "correctly" penalizing late steps that were already doomed by early failures — a form of misattributed credit that MRPO directly addresses. This is structurally the same problem as the SeL failure mode (Jul 5) but at the step level within a trajectory rather than at the trajectory level across episodes.

ReLook (0.72) operationalizes RLAIF in the agentic coding domain by using MLLM-generated visual critique as the reward signal rather than string-match correctness. The Forced Optimization acceptance rule (only improving revisions admitted) is the agentic coding analog of RLVR verifiable rewards — it replaces the binary pass/fail oracle with a monotonicity constraint, preventing the agent from exploring degenerate trajectories that happen to satisfy a lenient verifier. The training-inference decoupling is the key efficiency insight: the critic is needed for exploration during training but not for exploitation at inference.

**Emerging connection — dual-mode and mode-switching in RL:** AutoTool (tool/no-tool), RL-PLUS (on/off-policy), and Nemotron-Cascade 2 (domain-teacher switching) all implement meta-level mode switching in the RL training loop. The common pattern is: identify two distinct behavioral modes that each excel in different regimes, train with rewards that encourage appropriate mode selection, and validate that the composite system outperforms either mode alone. The RLVR community is converging on mode-switching as a general architectural primitive, not just a domain-specific design choice.
