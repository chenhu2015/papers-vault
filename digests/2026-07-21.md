The VLM faithfulness search (5) was by far the most productive — five papers all above threshold, with DyCo-RL and SIVA-RL both scoring ≥0.87. Today's digest is notable for being the first day with all five papers from the multimodal/VLM RL domain.

DyCo-RL (0.88) introduces a cross-modal coordination breakdown as a failure mode distinct from all previously identified VLM RL failures. CORA (Jul 19) caught thinking-answer inconsistency at the trace level; SIVA-RL (today) catches visual sensitivity gap at the sample level; DyCo-RL catches cross-modal attention misallocation at the token level. The Fisher-Rao geodesic distance for measuring within-modality attention shifts is also the first use of information-geometric measures at the token attention level in the vault — connecting the information geometry thread (RIPO's Riemannian PPO-Clip, Value-Gradient Hypothesis) to multimodal token-level credit assignment.

SIVA-RL (0.87) makes a key structural argument: existing visual-intervention methods (which contrast behavior on original vs. modified images) fail because they assign supervision by operator type, not by the observed effect on the reward. The same patch operation can produce heterogeneous outcomes across samples. SIVA-RL's outcome-conditioned routing resolves this by observing the actual reward drop and using it as a routing signal. This is the visual analogue to DISPO's insight: just as DISPO showed that the same IS clipping rule should not apply uniformly to correct and incorrect responses, SIVA-RL shows that the same intervention rule should not apply uniformly to sensitive and insensitive samples.

BetaPRM (0.83) adds a calibration dimension to the PRM thread that the vault had not previously addressed. The existing PRM papers (GroundedPRM, KV-PRM, SCI-PRM, FaithRL) all use PRMs as training signals without distinguishing confident from uncertain step predictions. BetaPRM's Beta distribution output makes the reliability explicit, enabling downstream methods to weight step rewards by trustworthiness. The Monte Carlo supervision protocol is practical and does not require additional annotation beyond what standard PRM training already requires.

FWS/Be Faithful (0.78) completes the VLM RL faithfulness picture by targeting the SFT initialization boundary: the other three papers (CORA, SIVA-RL, DyCo-RL) address failure modes during RL optimization; FWS addresses the failure mode that arises from the initialization itself (a policy that has not learned to look at visual evidence before RL begins). The FaithfulQA curation strategy — selecting samples with explicit vision-language causal relationships across five categories — is the most structured dataset contribution in the vault's VLM RL section.

H-GRPO (0.76) is the first paper in the vault to require explicit bounding-box evidence for each intermediate reasoning sub-step, making spatial visual grounding a necessary part of the reward structure (not just the final answer). The permutation-invariant GRPO formulation closes the option-ordering artifact gap in grounding tasks that ACRE (Jul 20) identified for VQA, confirming this artifact generalizes. The 4B outperforming 7B models is notable: explicit structural decomposition appears to be a stronger inductive bias for grounding than scale.

**New thread — VLM RL visual faithfulness (four distinct failure modes now characterized):**
1. **Language prior exploitation** (FWS): model defaults to linguistic patterns, ignoring visual input; fix: SFT warm-start with causally grounded VQA data
2. **Thinking-answer semantic inconsistency** (CORA): reasoning trace is coherent but semantically inconsistent with the final answer; fix: plug-and-play consistency reward model + HRAS
3. **Visual sensitivity gap** (SIVA-RL): answer-level correctness does not guarantee sensitivity to visual evidence; fix: outcome-conditioned PatchSwap supervision
4. **Cross-modal attention misallocation** (DyCo-RL): tokens fail to alternate between visual extraction and textual synthesis roles; fix: Fisher-Rao attention-role alignment-guided advantage reweighting

**Updated PRM thread:** GroundedPRM, KV-PRM, SCI-PRM (process supervision methods), LLMs-as-a-Jury (evaluation), Paradox of Outcome Optimization (theoretical necessity), FaithRL (faithfulness-specific PRM as auxiliary reward), BetaPRM (calibrated reliability estimation for step-level PRM output). BetaPRM adds the calibration dimension — the thread now covers *what* to supervise (GroundedPRM/KV-PRM/SCI-PRM), *why* it's necessary (Paradox), *when* to apply it (FaithRL: faithfulness failures), and *how reliably* the PRM signal should be weighted (BetaPRM).
