# GUI Agents with Reinforcement Learning: Toward Digital Inhabitants

## Summary

First comprehensive survey of RL applied to GUI agents, providing a taxonomy of Offline/Online/Hybrid RL approaches. Highlights key trends: composite reward architectures, world-model training to overcome I/O latency bottlenecks, and emergent System-2 deliberation without explicit supervision.

## Key Contributions

- Taxonomy of RL approaches for GUI agents: Offline / Online / Hybrid
- Survey of composite reward architectures used in GUI agent training
- Analysis of world-model training as a solution to environment I/O latency
- Emergent System-2 reasoning observed without explicit supervision signals

## Relevance

Useful survey connecting agentic RL to a concrete application domain (GUI automation). Relevant to tool-use and agentic RL threads; covers reward model design patterns applicable beyond GUI settings.
