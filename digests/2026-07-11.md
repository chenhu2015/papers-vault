TSR (0.84) is the training-time analog of ROSE's search-time semantic-entropy branching. Where ROSE branches during inference to explore diverse reasoning paths for a fixed problem, TSR branches during training rollout to build higher-quality multi-turn agent trajectories. The key design insight is repurposing test-time scaling ideas (MCTS, beam search) as a training-time data quality mechanism — making tree search available for training without inference-time overhead. This extends the rollout diversity thread (TA-GRPO, Reaching Beyond the Mode, ROSE) with a new mechanism operating at the trajectory construction level.

DART (0.83) is the tool-integrated complement to ARTIST (Jul 09: pipeline-level tool selection) and DynaWeb (Jul 09: world model for tool environments). DART addresses a third gap: *where within a long CoT to insert tool calls*, which neither ARTIST nor DynaWeb addresses. The dynamic rollout tree construction is also the structural complement to TSR: TSR branches on state-based scores to diversify *agent actions across turns*, while DART branches on promising CoT positions to diversify *tool invocation decisions within a reasoning chain*. Together they complete a two-level tree-search picture for agentic RL: cross-turn (TSR) and within-turn (DART).

PRISM (0.82) is the first multimodal-specific paper in the vault to address SFT-to-RL distributional drift as a distinct problem from the text-only case. The key insight is that multimodal drift is *compound*: perception errors and reasoning errors follow distinct failure patterns that interact and amplify during RLVR. The MoE discriminator with separate perception/reasoning branches is a direct structural response to this compound failure — it provides disentangled corrective signals that align each modality before they interact in RL training. The result: +4.4 to +6.0 points over SFT-to-RLVR baseline, consistent across three RL objectives (GRPO, DAPO, GSPO).

SaGe (0.80) adds the scene-graph structured representation paradigm to the vault's VLM RL thread. The node-as-proxy graph reward is the visual counterpart of SR-GRPO's internal-geometry reward (Jul 10): both use structured intermediate representations as verifiable reward signals rather than flat scalar outcomes. SaGe's navigational failure mode (MLLMs focus on isolated objects, ignoring relational structure) is also the visual analog of the exploratory primitive suppression failure mode (Jul 10, "When RL Suppresses Its Own Vocabulary"): in both cases, RL without structured rewards narrows the model's behavioral vocabulary.

OSPO (0.78) adds a fifth sub-sequence credit assignment approach to the vault: game-theoretic Shapley-Owen attribution. The vault now has five approaches operating at five different theoretical foundations: MRPO (temporal position heuristic), GRPO-λ (TD eligibility traces), EGSPO (entropy-gated routing), ROSE's length-aware segment advantage (length-normalized segment credit), and OSPO (cooperative game theory, Shapley-Owen). OSPO is the only one with formal axiomatic justification (efficiency, symmetry, dummy, additivity) — the other four are principled but heuristic. The potential-based reward shaping formulation also preserves policy-optimality in a way the others do not explicitly guarantee.
