Query 4 (distillation) returned mostly overlapping results with query 3. The most productive angle was contrastive/discriminative reward (query 2), yielding 3 of 5 papers.

SCPO (0.84) is the standout: it identifies semantic credit inconsistency as a structural failure of group-based RL distinct from zero-gradient (ARBOR Jun 19) and probability squeezing (SPS Jun 20). The sibling-credit recovery mechanism is the cleanest complement to STRIDE (Jun 20, discriminative n-gram contrast for successful trajectories) — STRIDE differentiates credit among successful trajectories by n-gram contrast, SCPO differentiates credit *within* failed trajectories by finding their successful analogs. Together STRIDE + SCPO + DRE + ProFIL (Jun 19-20) form a complete credit recovery stack operating at four distinct failure modes.

EvoRubrics (0.84) completes the rubric-based reward pipeline with the missing temporal dimension: ARES (Jun 19) synthesizes rubrics offline from pretraining data, ARBOR (Jun 19) accumulates rubrics online during training, and EvoRubrics makes the rubric generator itself an adversarially trained co-evolver that stays ahead of the policy. This closes the Goodhart loop: static rubrics (ARES/ARBOR) saturate as the policy improves, just as static reward models Goodhart under RLHF. The self-supervised variant achieving meaningful gains suggests that evaluation criteria for any domain can be bootstrapped purely from generation-evaluation co-evolution without domain data.

ExTra (0.82) unifies and operationalizes the exploration thread by targeting both difficulty extremes within one framework. Its entropy-guided prefix regeneration is the most direct application of the ReasonMaxxer finding (Jun 20): instead of analyzing that RL modifies only high-entropy positions, ExTra explicitly exploits those positions as branching points for directed exploration. The novelty reward addresses the complementary homogeneity failure (SPS Jun 20's probability squeezing) without IRL retraining.

GraphGPO (0.81) introduces the fourth axis of the credit assignment hierarchy: cross-trajectory goal-distance estimation via unified state graph. The hierarchy now covers: token-level (AT-RL Jun 14), trajectory-segment (DRE Jun 19), action-type (HiMPO Jun 18), sibling-step (SCPO today), and cross-trajectory reachability (GraphGPO today). Notably, SCPO and GraphGPO are orthogonal: SCPO uses *semantically similar* successful siblings for step credit, GraphGPO uses *any* trajectories that passed through a state and tracks global distance to goal. Both attack failed-trajectory credit loss but with different state representations and distance metrics.

SWITCH (0.80) provides the mechanistic bridge between latent reasoning and the sparse-position RL framework: the <swi> boundary token is the explicit discretized form of the high-entropy commitment position that ReasonMaxxer (Jun 20) identified as the site of RL intervention in visible reasoning. This makes SWITCH not just an engineering contribution (GRPO-compatible latent reasoning) but also a mechanistic confirmation: RL intervention in *any* reasoning mode — visible (AT-RL, ReasonMaxxer) or latent (SWITCH) — concentrates at single discrete commitment moments.
