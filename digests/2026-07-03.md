G2PO (0.91) is the structural standout of the digest. While GraphGPO (Jun 30) and RewardFlow (Jul 2) each layered graph-based signals on top of a separate training algorithm, G2PO integrates the state-transition graph directly into the GRPO advantage estimator: the group-aggregation state-value estimation is exactly what a "GRPO with a graph-aware baseline" would look like, and the edge-centric globally standardized TD error is the advantage counterpart to RewardFlow's retrospective propagation. G2PO thus closes the loop opened by GraphGPO: not just "use the graph for a better reward signal" but "use the graph to build a better GRPO advantage estimator from scratch."

BiPACE (0.90) is the most principled response to the singleton-group problem that has appeared implicitly in several prior papers (CRAFT's G-1 sibling rollouts, TRIAGE's role-typed grouping, DuCA's dual-horizon normalization all workaround it but don't name it). BiPACE names it precisely: observation-hash partitioning creates singleton groups with zero per-step signal because the same abstract state appears under slightly different surface observations. Bisimulation clustering via the actor's own hidden-state geometry (a policy-induced bisimulation proxy) is the principled fix — it collapses to the same behavioral equivalence class that TRIAGE's role taxonomy approximates by hand, but without requiring taxonomy design.

TAPO (0.88) completes the tool-use credit picture that SGCD opens: where SGCD uses sibling contrast (external LLM summarization of success/failure difference) to build a credit reference, TAPO uses parameter-determinism to construct counterfactual witnesses within the current training batch — no external model needed. The >50% misassignment statistic for failing tool-use trajectories under GRPO's uniform broadcast is the most concrete quantification of the tool-use credit problem in the vault to date. TAPO also fills the multimodal credit gap: the VLM search agent setting makes it the only paper in today's digest that lands on the VLM axis despite search 4 returning empty.

SGCD (0.85) establishes the design rule that distinguishes it from both CRAFT and TAPO: distillation should guide credit weighting, but policy gradient must remain the sole authority over actor parameters. This is the tool-use extension of CRAFT's self-distillation architecture (where CRAFT used importance-reweighted sibling tokens, SGCD uses LLM-contrast-summarized sibling credit) applied to AppWorld/tau^3 tasks where trajectory length makes per-token reweighting too noisy.

UCOB (0.82) introduces a credit assignment problem not previously addressed in the vault: when should the policy trust its retrieved skill memory vs. its own in-context reasoning? The return-to-go comparison at anchor states is credit assignment across context views rather than across rollout steps — a novel axis that complements the six structural axes in the prior credit hierarchy (rollout state graph, attention DAG, sibling-step, trajectory-segment, token-level, role-typed segment).

**Updated credit assignment hierarchy (7 axes):** rollout state graph (GraphGPO/G2PO), attention DAG (FlowTracer), sibling-step (SCPO/CRAFT/SGCD), trajectory-segment (DRE/TRIAGE), token-level (AT-RL/TAPO), role-typed segment (TRIAGE), context-view (UCOB). BiPACE operates at the state-partition level (bisimulation clusters) which sits orthogonally to all seven — it redefines what "same state" means for any of the above.
