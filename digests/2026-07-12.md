**All 5 papers from searches 1 and 4.** Search 2 was empty, search 3 was mostly off-target. The RL+TTS synergy query (search 4) was the most productive new direction. The tree-PRM query (search 1) confirmed HRM and GroundedPRM as the main answers to the question opened by DART.

RL^V (0.88) is the most significant paper today. It identifies a structural flaw in all value-free RL methods (GRPO, LooPPO): by discarding the learned value function, they block the test-time compute scaling that requires a verifier. The generative verifier co-training restores this capability using data that RL training already produces — no extra annotation, no separate reward model. The result is not just a performance boost (+20% MATH) but a qualitative capability shift: the RL-trained model can now leverage parallel and sequential TTS at 8-32x efficiency. This places RL^V as the bridge between the vault's RL training thread and the test-time compute scaling thread.

T1 (0.83) addresses the same question from the training side: what training design makes a model that benefits from more inference compute? The answer is rollout diversity via oversampling — not a verifier, but a richer solution space learned during training. This complements RL^V: RL^V provides the mechanism to use test-time compute (generative verifier), T1 trains models that have the internal diversity to benefit from it. Together they form a complete picture: T1 trains diverse reasoners, RL^V trains evaluators to select among them.

HRM (0.82) is the tree-based PRM answer to the gap opened by DART. Standard PRMs evaluate steps independently and fail on self-correction sequences (where a flawed step is later corrected). HRM evaluates consecutive step pairs at two granularities: fine (individual step) and coarse (step pair). The hierarchical granularity mirrors DART's two-level tree-search picture (cross-turn TSR, within-turn DART): HRM's fine-grained evaluation maps to within-turn tool-insertion scoring, and coarse-grained evaluation maps to cross-turn trajectory quality. MCTS-generated training data with HNC augmentation also closes the loop with GroundedPRM: both use MCTS to generate PRM training data, but HRM compresses steps via HNC while GroundedPRM verifies steps via external tools.

Video-RTS (0.82) is the VLM extension of the RL+TTS synergy. The finding that pure RL without SFT — trained on 3.6% of the data — outperforms fully-supervised models is the video counterpart of PRISM's three-stage pipeline result (SFT is necessary for text models but costly for video). The sparse-to-dense TTS is the most novel contribution: it adaptively allocates temporal context at inference time by adding frames when outputs are inconsistent, which is the video analog of ROSE's semantic-entropy branching — both use consistency signals to guide adaptive inference-time compute allocation.

GroundedPRM (0.80) completes the tree-PRM thread. The tool-verification component is the most direct connection to the agentic tool-use framework (AutoTool, ARTIST, DynaWeb, DART): GroundedPRM uses tool execution as a ground-truth verifier for step correctness. This is the reward modeling complement to DART's tool-insertion discovery: DART finds where to insert tool calls in a long CoT via tree rollouts; GroundedPRM can verify whether those insertions were correct via execution. Together they could form a unified agentic RL system where DART discovers tool opportunities and GroundedPRM scores them.

**Emerging framework — RL + inference scaling synergy (new):** Three papers now form the vault's first explicit RL+TTS framework: RL^V (co-train a generative verifier to use test-time compute), T1 (oversampling diversity during RL training to unlock inference scaling behavior), and Video-RTS (pure RL + sparse-to-dense adaptive TTS for video). The key insight across all three: RL training must be co-designed with test-time compute usage, not treated as independent phases.

**Emerging framework — tree-based process reward (updated):** DART (Jul 11) introduced tree-based process advantage as a training signal for policy optimization. Today, HRM and GroundedPRM add the PRM side: tree-structured reward models that can supervise those policy updates. The complementary pairing is: GroundedPRM (MCTS + tool-verification → fine-grained PRM) provides step-level reward for DART's tree rollouts; HRM (hierarchical consecutive-step evaluation) provides coherence-aware reward for TSR's multi-turn trajectory trees.
