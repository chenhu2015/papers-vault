**All 3 papers from Searches 3 and 4.** Searches 1, 2, 5 empty/off-target. The SFT-free VLM RL search (4) was the most productive despite targeting a different gap — it surfaced GFlowRL as a new agentic RL thread paper via query drift.

GFlowRL (0.83) introduces distribution-matching as a fundamental alternative to reward-maximizing RL for LLMs. The key scaling insight — that the learned partition function is the bottleneck at large scale, not the GFlowNet objective itself — parallels BPO's insight that independent rollouts are the bottleneck in agentic RL variance reduction (not the GRPO estimator). In both cases, the fix is structural: BPO removes prefix redundancy; GFlowRL removes the auxiliary network. The asymmetric flow-gap clipping is structurally identical to DISPO's asymmetric IS clipping — both suppress outlier gradients without zeroing them, from different motivations (flow balance vs. importance weight variance). The diversity-encouraging objective directly addresses the mode collapse failure mode from DEPT's self-play thread and the headroom deficit from the GRPO web agent failure analysis: GFlowNet RL maintains diversity pressure even when the policy has high headroom over sampling, potentially sidestepping the "sampling ≤ greedy → no gradient signal" failure mode.

MADA-RL (0.77) partially closes the self-play reasoning gap (confirmed sparse, 2 prior searches). The counterfactual critic advantage — critic reward minus generator ensemble accuracy — is a form of counterfactual credit assignment at the inter-agent level. CRAFT (Jul 2) computed counterfactual baselines at the trajectory prefix level; BDA (Jul 17) at the turn level; MADA-RL at the role level. The gains are modest (+2.0pp on 1.5B at 16x parameter reduction), and the math-only evaluation leaves the agentic/code self-play gap open.

Masked Diffusion World Models (0.75) provides a complementary angle on agentic RL diversity. Where BPO achieves diversity within a single real-environment rollout via prefix branching, and GFlowRL achieves diversity via distribution-matching objectives, MDLMs achieve diversity via bidirectional conditioning during world model rollout generation. The three papers form a coherent cluster of "anti-mode-collapse" strategies: structural diversity (BPO), objective diversity (GFlowRL), and generative diversity (MDLMs). The tool schema conditioning connects directly to the interest profile's tool use and LLM agent axes.

**New thread — GFlowNet-style distribution-matching RL for LLMs:** GFlowRL establishes scalable GFlowNet RL as a viable post-training alternative to PPO/GRPO. The reward-distribution-matching objective (match the distribution over trajectories weighted by reward, not just maximize expected reward) is the strongest alternative to reward-maximizing RL surfaced in the vault — stronger than the entropy-regularized variants (EGRSD, RL-ZVP) which modify GRPO's gradient but retain reward-maximization.
